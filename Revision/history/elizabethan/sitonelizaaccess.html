The situation on Elizabeth's accession

When Elizabeth became Queen after Mary died in 1558, she was faced with many urgent problems such as foreign challenges. She also faced the controversy over her vulnerability and ability as a Queen.

Elizabethan England in 1558

The structure of society in England was heirarchical and this was aso seen in the way that the country was governed. Each group in society knew its place and obeyed those who were above them in the hierarchy.

Society and Government

The queen was the head of society and government and was seen as deriving her position and power from God. She made all the major decisions. The Royal court was the centre of all political power in Elizabeth's reign. Her chief advisers and eky government officials all attended the court.
